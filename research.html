Research

We chose this topic because we believe that many people's mental health is being negatively affected by the extensive use of social media from an early age.

Researchers observed a sharp increase in social media usage among young people during the 2020 pandemic. This trend raises concerns about its impact on mental health.

Key Findings:

  • Cyberbullying and Mental Health Issues: Social media can expose users, especially children and teens, to cyberbullying and feelings of inadequacy, leading to anxiety and depression.
  • Privacy Concerns: Many young users are unaware of how their data is collected and used, putting their privacy at risk.
  • Addiction and Distraction: Constant use of social media can become addictive and distract students from schoolwork or other productive activities.
  • Disruption of Healthy Behaviors: According to Dr. Murthy's advisory, excessive social media use can disrupt important healthy behaviors in teens.

Length of time online

This study also looked at the link between the length of time spent on social media and your mental health.
It found that the positive effects of social media stayed constant regardless of how much time spent online.
However, as the time spent online increased mental health was negatively effected more and more.

Negative Impact of Social Media on Mental Health

Younger generations reported that social media has a bigger negatice impact on their mental health than older generations.
Worldwide, 18% of respondants under 27 stated that social media had a negative impact on their mental health.
However, in Ireland that figure was significantly higher at 24%.
