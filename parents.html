Parents Guide to a Better Internet

Webwise have an excellent guide for parents on their website, which we have summarised below.
If you would like to view the full document, and other helpful resources for parents please click here

Summary

  • Understanding the Online World:
    The internet is integral to children's lives, offering opportunities to learn, create, and connect. Parents are encouraged to engage with their children's online activities to better understand their experiences.
  • Open Communication:
    Maintaining open and ongoing conversations about internet use helps children feel comfortable discussing their online experiences, including any negative encounters. This approach fosters trust and ensures children seek parental guidance when needed.
  • Social Media Awareness:
    Parents should discuss the use of social media with their children, emphasizing the importance of privacy settings and the potential risks of sharing personal information. Encouraging children to think critically about their online interactions is crucial. .
  • Managing Screen Time:
    Establishing balanced screen time is essential for healthy development. The guide suggests setting agreed-upon limits and encouraging offline activities to ensure a well-rounded lifestyle.
  • Addressing Cyberbullying:
    Parents are advised to be vigilant for signs of cyberbullying and to educate their children on how to respond to and report such incidents. Understanding the platforms their children use can help parents provide appropriate support.
  • Online Privacy and Data Protection:
    Teaching children about the importance of protecting their personal data online is vital. Parents should guide them on using privacy settings and understanding their data protection rights.
